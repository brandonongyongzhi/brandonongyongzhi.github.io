Caring for someone with Dementia

Set Routines

Try to keep a routine, such as bathing and eating at the same time each day. Write down to-do lists, appointments, and events.

Encourage Independence

When dressing or bathing, allow the person to do as much as possible. This help to maintain muscle memory through enabling independence.

Be Gentle and Respectful

Tell the person what you are going to do, step by step while you help them and guide them through tasks. Patience is key.

Where to Get Help

Please seek help from professionals to get a proper diagnosis!

Do I have Dementia?


Take this quiz to find out if you have symptoms of Dementia

This quiz is NOT a diagnostic tool. Mental health disorders can only be diagnosed by licensed healthcare professionals.


Q1: Do you have trouble focusing or concentrating when completing activites





Q2: Do you get easily confused driving, or using tools? Do you get lost in places that are familiar to you (i.e. your neighborhood or the grocery store)?





Q3: Do you get confused with recalling the day of the week, month, year, important dates, and/or do you repeat yourself in conversations?





Q4: Do you need help getting dressed, remembering to take medication, and/or handling your finances?





Q5: Are these difficulties getting worse?
